Photography Tips & Tricks

Lighting Is Everything

Tap to flip

When shooting food, natural light is your best friend. Position your dish near a window but avoid direct sunlight. Use a white foam board to bounce light and fill shadows on the opposite side.

Find Your Angle

Tap to flip

Every dish has its perfect angle. Flat foods like pizza look best from above (90°), while layered dishes like burgers shine from a 45° angle. Experiment to find what showcases the food's best features.

Color Theory

Tap to flip

Use complementary colors to make your food pop. A green garnish on a red tomato soup creates visual interest. Choose plates and backgrounds that enhance, not compete with, your food's natural colors.

Composition Matters

Tap to flip

Apply the rule of thirds to your food photos. Place your main subject at intersection points rather than center. Create depth by having elements in foreground, middle ground, and background.

Subtle Editing

Tap to flip

Less is more with food photo editing. Slightly increase contrast, selectively brighten key highlights, and enhance vibrance rather than saturation. Maintain natural colors that make viewers hungry!

Food Styling Secrets

Tap to flip

Use tweezers for precise placement of small elements. Brush oils on meat for shine. Undercook pasta slightly so it looks firmer. Add fresh herbs at the last moment. Keep a spray bottle with water for refreshing wilted ingredients.
